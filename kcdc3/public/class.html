Sharing the Knowledge

Date: Thursday, 17 January 2013

Time: 6:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.

Instructor: Julia Goren

Location: Southeast Library

Ever been to a class so compelling that it seemed over in a flash — and yet you remember everything that was taught? Or sat through a course that seemed to last an eternity? Chances are the subject matter was less important to your experience than the instructor's ability.

Explore the theory and techniques that will help improve your instruction in this interactive class. We'll examine, discuss, and play with ideas about teaching and learning.

This class is designed for those teaching KCDC courses who want to build their skills and repertoire of instruction techniques. It is also appropriate for anyone who teaches or gives public presentations in any capacity.

Location

Southeast Library
403 7th St. SE
Washington, DC 20003
Neighborhood: Capitol Hill
Across the street from the Eastern Market Metro.

Instructor

Julia Goren has been a student for much of the past 34 years. She's learned from teachers both terrible and outstanding, and has spent time thinking about each. She has an M.S. in environmental education and is a certified Interpretive Trainer and Guide through the National Association for Interpretation. She enjoys hiking, alpine plants, and visiting balmy D.C. from her home in the snowy Adirondacks.
